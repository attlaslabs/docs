Attlas is designed to help you organize different projects and collaborate with others.

With **workspaces**, you can separate your chats into different environments. With **teams**, you can invite people to view or edit specific chats.

Together, these features help keep your work organized, secure, and scalable.

---

## Workspaces

**Workspaces** act as independent environments for organizing your chats.

Think of them as folders or separate workspaces for different projects.

<Frame>
  ![Workspace](/images/workspace.jpg)
</Frame>

## Why use Workspaces?

Workspaces help you separate clients, projects, teams, or areas of work.

### Examples

#### Agencies and freelancers

You can create:

- One workspace for each client;
- One workspace for internal projects;
- One workspace for testing and experiments.

#### Content creators

You can separate:

- Public chats for your audience;
- Course and training chats;
- Personal projects;
- Professional projects.

#### Businesses

You can organize:

- Customer support;
- Marketing;
- Internal training;
- Knowledge bases.

---

## How to create a new workspace

To create a new workspace:

1. Click the Workspace selector in the sidebar
2. Click **<kbd>Add new workspace</kbd>**
3. Choose a name for the new workspace
4. Start creating chats inside it.

Each workspace has its own chats and organization.

This makes it easier to manage multiple projects without mixing different types of content.

## Teams

In addition to organizing your chats, you can also collaborate with other people.

Attlas allows you to invite members to view or edit specific chats.

![Screenshot Studio 1781029213801](/images/screenshot-studio-1781029213801.jpg)

## How to invite members

To invite someone to a chat:

1. Open the desired chat;
2. Go to **Settings → Access**;
3. Scroll down to the **Chat Members** section;
4. Enter the person's email address;
5. Select the appropriate permission level;
6. Click **Send Invitation**.

The user will receive an invitation to access the chat.

---

## Permission types

When inviting a member, you can define their level of access.

### Editor

Can:

- Edit the chat;
- Manage content;
- Change settings;
- Collaborate on chat development.

Ideal for team members actively involved in building and maintaining the chat.

### Viewer

Can:

- View the chat;
- Follow content updates;
- Review information.

Cannot make changes.

Ideal for clients, managers, or anyone who only needs visibility into the project.

---

## Important: invitations are chat-based

Members are not added to an entire Workspace.

Each invitation is linked to a specific chat.

This means:

- A user may have access to one chat;
- While not having access to other chats within the same Workspace.

This approach provides greater flexibility and control over team management.

---

## Chat access control

<Check>
  Inside `Settings → Access`, you will also find the chat visibility options.
</Check>

You can choose whether your chat is:

- **Public:** Anyone with the link can access the chat.
- **Password Protected:** Visitors must enter a password before accessing the chat.
- **Invite Only:** Only invited users can access the chat.
- **Private:** Only you can access the chat.

These options help you control exactly who can view and interact with your content.

## Better organization for projects and teams

Workspaces help you organize projects.

Teams help you collaborate.

When used together, these features allow you to create structured environments, share responsibilities, and maintain full control over who can access each chat.
